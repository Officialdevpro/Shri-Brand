The Paradox of Sacred Waste

During his tenure as Managing Trustee at Shri Rajaganapathi Temple, Sri Suganvaneswara Temple, and Sri Kashi Vishwanathar Temple in Salem, Shri C Valliappa observed an alarming reality. The daily rituals of temple worship generated enormous quantities of fresh flowers that, despite their purity and fragrance, were destined for landfills.

The Critical Challenge

These flowers, blessed by centuries of spiritual tradition, were treated as waste products rather than valuable resources. The cognitive dissonance between honoring the divine and discarding these offerings troubled his conscience, propelling him to seek solutions that could align spiritual values with environmental responsibility.

Environmental Impact

Thousands of tons of organic waste generated daily from temples across India contribute to:

  • Landfill burden and methane emissions
  • Loss of valuable organic resources
  • Environmental degradation
  • Missed circular economy opportunities

Social-Economic Gap

The waste represented a missed opportunity for:

  • Sustainable value creation
  • Rural women's employment
  • Economic empowerment initiatives
  • Community development programs

Design Thinking Meets Mentorship

Recognizing the magnitude of the challenge, Shri C Valliappa mentored Dr M E Paramasivam, a faculty member at Sona College of Technology, whose expertise in business analysis and market research proved invaluable. Together, they embarked on an extensive exploration using design thinking methodology.

Problem Definition

Identified the paradox of sacred offerings becoming environmental waste while rural women lacked economic opportunities.

Market Research

Dr Paramasivam's analysis revealed that dried temple flowers could serve as premium raw material for agarbathi (incense) production.

Solution Prototyping

Developed circular economy model aligning with India's sustainability vision and growing consumer demand for eco-friendly products.

Mentorship Catalyst

Shri Valliappa mentored Mrs G C Saranya, transforming her from returning professional to social entrepreneur.

The Transformative Mentorship

The turning point came when Shri C Valliappa discussed this market opportunity with Mrs G C Saranya, an MCA graduate and software professional who had stepped away to embrace motherhood. Rather than encouraging conventional employment, he mentored her toward entrepreneurship‚Äîspecifically, social entrepreneurship that combined personal agency with community impact.

"True mentorship is not about directing people back to familiar paths‚Äîit's about illuminating new possibilities that align personal potential with societal need."

Arudhra Creations: From Concept to Reality

In August 2025, Arudhra Creations Pvt Limited was formally established as a women-led rural startup dedicated to manufacturing agarbathi from temple-blessed flowers. The name "Arudhra," synonymous with compassion and sacred devotion, encapsulates the enterprise's dual mission: honoring spiritual tradition while creating tangible economic value.

Product Innovation

  • Sacred raw material from post-worship flower collection
  • Artisanal processing using traditional drying methods
  • Zero-waste manufacturing with composted byproducts
  • Premium positioning with cultural authenticity
  • Natural fragrance and therapeutic properties

Institutional Recognition

  • Faculty startup under NEP 2020 framework
  • Sona Incubation Foundation support
  • Aligned with Startup India Initiative
  • Women-led enterprise certification
  • Sustainable Development Goals compliance

Circular Economy in Action

The agarbathi produced by Arudhra Creations embodies sustainable entrepreneurship principles. Every aspect of the production process‚Äîfrom sacred flower collection to artisanal processing to zero-waste byproduct management‚Äîdemonstrates practical implementation of circular economy ideals. Market validation through EXPO events and preliminary sales have demonstrated strong consumer acceptance among temple devotees, spirituality enthusiasts, and eco-conscious consumers.
